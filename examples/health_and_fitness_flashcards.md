# Health and Fitness - Flashcards
Generated on 2025-11-25 20:27

Q1: What is the difference between muscular strength and muscular endurance?
A1: Muscular strength is the maximum force a muscle can exert, while muscular endurance is the ability of a muscle to repeatedly exert force over time.

Q2: Explain the concept of progressive overload in strength training.
A2: Progressive overload is gradually increasing the demands on your muscles (weight, reps, sets) over time to stimulate muscle growth and strength gains.

Q3: What are some key differences between HIIT (High-Intensity Interval Training) and steady-state cardio?
A3: HIIT involves short bursts of intense exercise followed by brief recovery periods, while steady-state cardio maintains a consistent level of exertion for a longer duration. HIIT is typically more time-efficient and can improve both aerobic and anaerobic fitness.

Q4: Describe the role of macronutrients (protein, carbohydrates, fats) in a balanced diet for fitness.
A4: Protein builds and repairs tissues; carbohydrates provide energy; fats are essential for hormone production and nutrient absorption. A balance supports overall health and fitness goals.

Q5: What are some benefits of incorporating flexibility training (e.g., stretching, yoga) into a fitness routine?
A5: Improved range of motion, reduced risk of injury, decreased muscle soreness, and better posture are key benefits of flexibility training.

Q6: How does sleep quality and quantity impact athletic performance and recovery?
A6: Insufficient sleep impairs muscle recovery, reduces energy levels, decreases cognitive function, and increases the risk of injuries, negatively impacting athletic performance.

Q7: What are some strategies for managing stress and its potential impact on fitness and health?
A7: Strategies include mindfulness, meditation, yoga, spending time in nature, maintaining a healthy social support system, and engaging in enjoyable activities.

Q8: How can hydration affect exercise performance and overall health?
A8: Dehydration can lead to decreased strength, stamina, and cognitive function, as well as increased risk of muscle cramps. Proper hydration is crucial for optimal performance and overall health.
